8 Principles of good data science

Written by Joe Tenini on September 19, 2022

When I first started working as a data scientist, I would start each day by writing one of Dieter Rams’s ten principles for good design down and sticking it on my desk. Taking inspiration from these (and stealing some), I’ll propose some principles of good data science:

  1. Good data science is honest — it knows its limits and does not promise to do things it cannot.
  2. Good data science is principled — design decisions justified by “because it works” are not as good as those justified by first principles or systematic empirical comparison of approaches.
  3. Good data science is designed with the application in mind — the goal is very rarely to create a model, it is usually to solve a problem. When we design with an application in mind we allow ourselves to be principled — guided by the eventual user.
  4. Good data science brings stakeholders along for the ride — data science solutions exist within an ecosystem. When we fail to involve stakeholders in the design process, we ignore this ecosystem and create unintended consequences in the environment.
  5. Good data science is innovative — our data science solutions strive to add differentiated value through differentiated data or design. We strive to avoid lazy thinking and incorporate diverse methodologies.
  6. Good data science is non-toxic — our data science solutions consider their broader impact both internally and externally and we strive to create a world that we want to live in.
  7. Good data science is collaborative — the best data science comes from designing with diverse perspectives. This is enabled by creating an inclusive environment where we prioritize empathy, kindness, and curiosity.
  8. Good data science is sustainable — data science should strive to be repeatable, maintainable, and portable. The best ideas can fit on a napkin, and the best solutions can be understood, monitored, and owned by anyone. We do not strive to build ice sculptures.

Maybe you don't like some (or all!) of these principles, and hopefully you have others that you like. Whatever the case, I would recommend data scientists to think about, write down, and live by their own principles of good data science.
